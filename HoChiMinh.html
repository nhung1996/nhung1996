Cuộc đời chủ tịch Hồ Chí Minh

Ho Chi Minh (19 May 1890 - 2 September 1969) birth name: Nguyen Sinh Cung, revolutionary, founder of the Communist Party Vietnam, one who laid the foundations and led the struggle for independence, Vietnam's territorial integrity in the twentieth century, a communist soldier international. He wrote and read the Declaration of Independence of Vietnam Democratic Republic of Vietnam on September 2, 1945 in Ba Dinh Square, Hanoi, President of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam during 1945-1969, Chairman of the Central Executive Committee of the Vietnam Workers' Party in The period 1951 - 1969.

In 1895, Nguyen Sinh Cung accompanied his parents and brother to Hue for the first time. in 1906, Nguyen Sinh Cung followed his father into Hue for the second time and attended school French-Vietnamese primary school in Dong Ba. June 5, 1911, from Nha Rong Wharf, N Van Ba, en route to France with a kitchen assistant on the ship Admiral Latouche-Tréville, Nguyen Ai Quoc arrived in the Soviet Union for the first time in 1922 to join The Fourth International Congress of the Communists, where he met Lenin and became the city member of the Communist International Committee of South East Asia.

Return to Vietnam

In early January 1941, Hoang Van Thu, Standing Member of the Party Central Committee from Vietnam to Tinh Tay (Quang Tay, China) met Nguyen Ai Quoc to report on the results of building and strengthening the Cao Bang area. Hoang Van Thu asked him to return directly to Vietnam through the Cao Bang. Nguyen Ai Quoc also stated that Cao Bang would open up great prospects for revolution, and he decided to return home after 30 years abroad.
